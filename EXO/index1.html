CYBER BULLYING

Cyberbullying refers to the use of digital communication tools, such as social media, messaging apps, email, or websites, to harass, threaten, intimidate, or demean individuals. It can take various forms, including text messages, images, videos, or rumors spread online with the
intention of causing harm.

Targets: Anyone, regardless of age, can become a target of cyberbullying. However, children and teenagers are particularly vulnerable due to their frequent use of digital devices and social media platforms.

Forms of Cyberbullying: Common forms of cyberbullying include sending hurtful or threatening messages, spreading false rumors, sharing embarrassing photos or videos, impersonating someone online, and excluding individuals from online groups or activities.

Consequences: Cyberbullying can have devastating consequences for victims, including emotional distress, depression, anxiety,
low self-esteem, and even suicidal thoughts. It can also impact academic performance and social relationships.

ONLINE GROOMING

Online grooming refers to the process by which an adult or older individual establishes trust and emotional connections with a child or adolescent through online communication platforms. The ultimate goal is to exploit the child, often for sexual purposes, but also for other forms of manipulation, coercion, or harm.

Methods: Groomers use various tactics to gain a child's trust, including flattery, gifts, compliments, and the creation of a false persona or identity. They may pretend to share common interests and sympathize with the child's problems to build rapport.

Consequences: Online grooming can lead to serious harm to the child, including emotional trauma, sexual exploitation, blackmail, and exposure to explicit content. It may also result in legal consequences for the groomer.
